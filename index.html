4-day course breakdown

Each cohort runs Wednesday to Saturday, 09:00–17:00, with a drinks reception on Saturday.

1

Day 1 — The theory of smile design

Photography, case planning, smile sketching, Digital Smile Design, 3D printing and palatal stent fabrication; pink aesthetics review; material selection; occlusal planning; placement of palatal shells.

DSD3D printingPalatal stentOcclusion
2

Day 2 — Layering and tooth anatomy

Anterior composite placement: tooth preparation, isolation, bonding, shaping, layering and creation of your first ten-unit case on a normal typodont; predictably recreating anatomy.

IsolationBondingShaping10-unit case
3

Day 3 — Texturing and polishing your veneers

Refine tooth planes and line angles; perfect surface texture and polish; finish your first ten-unit case and begin placement on the space-closure case.

Line anglesTexturePolishSpace closure
4

Day 4 — Creating a masterpiece

Independent polishing; photographing/documenting your work; placement, polishing and photography of a final ten-unit veneer case on a tooth wear patient; review + certificates + drinks reception.

DocumentationPhotographyTooth wearCertificates

Is this for you?

This course is not an introduction to composite veneers. It is advanced and intensive training designed to refine an already stable skillset. If you are unsure, reach out and the team can advise.

Strong fit if you:

  • Already place composites and want higher-end anterior aesthetics.
  • Want predictable texture and polish protocols.
  • Need a workflow that holds under time pressure.
  • Want to improve clinical photography and presentation.
Enquire about suitability

Consider alternatives if you:

  • Have never layered anterior composites.
  • Need a fundamentals-first pace and more introductory demos.
  • Want an “intro to veneers” course rather than refinement training.

Policies and requirements

Key booking terms and equipment requirements (summarised from the official page).

Booking terms

  • Course fees are non-refundable.
  • To reschedule to another date, minimum 30 days’ notice is required.
  • Absence/sickness must be reported 24 hours before the day of the course.
  • No-notice non-attendance may incur a per-day fee (25% of course value) when rebooking.

For definitive terms, refer to the official page.

Equipment

  • A significant part of the course requires an iPad, Apple Pencil and a DSLR camera.
  • If you do not bring these, no substitute is provided.

Accreditation

  • The course is accredited.
  • Upon completion you receive 32 hours of CPD.
  • Certification cannot be given until all four days are completed.
