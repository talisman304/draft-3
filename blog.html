Why “natural” layered composites look different

A clinician-first explanation of anatomy cues: tooth planes, line angles, texture and controlled gloss.

“Natural” is rarely about one thing. It is the combined effect of macro form, micro texture, and how light transitions across surface planes. In layered composites, small positioning choices can create disproportionate visual differences.

1) Planes and line angles

If your planes are flat, your highlights are broad. If your line angles drift, symmetry appears “off” even when the incisal edge is correct. Build your shape intentionally, then refine the transitions.

2) Texture and gloss

Texture is the “noise” the eye expects on enamel. Gloss is the “camera” that reveals it. The goal is not maximum shine—it is believable shine.

3) Photographing what matters

Use consistent angles and lighting. Document intermediate stages: it improves patient understanding and lets you self-audit consistency.
